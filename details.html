Proposition

03.08.2023 Ostravice 8 checkpoints 48 hours

There are 8 obligatory checkpoints that everyone has to ride through. You will receive the list of checkpoints 2 days before beginning of the race. You have to create your own way to meet all the chechpoints. There is a time limit 48 hours to complete the whole route.

Rules

Self-sufficiency: Participants must be fully self-supported throughout the race. No outside assistance or support is allowed, including vehicle support, pre-arranged resupply, or accommodation.

Mandatory equipment: Each participant must carry essential equipment, including a helmet, bike repair tools, spare tubes, a first aid kit, a mobile phone, navigation devices, and appropriate clothing for varying weather conditions.

Respect for nature: Riders must respect the natural environment and wildlife. No littering is permitted, and participants should stay on designated trails, avoiding any damage to flora or fauna.

Non-drafting: Drafting behind other riders or motor vehicles is strictly prohibited. Participants must maintain a safe distance from other racers, except during brief overtaking maneuvers.

Navigation: Participants are responsible for navigation and must follow the designated race course. GPS devices, maps, or other approved navigational tools may be used, but pre-marking or altering the course is not allowed.

Time limits: There may be time limits imposed on specific sections or checkpoints to ensure the race progresses within a reasonable timeframe. Participants must adhere to these time limits to continue in the race.

Safety first: Participants must prioritize safety at all times. This includes obeying traffic rules, using caution on descents and technical sections, and yielding to pedestrians or other trail users.

Fair play: All participants are expected to exhibit fair play, sportsmanship, and respect towards fellow racers, volunteers, organizers, and the local community. Unsportsmanlike conduct or cheating will result in disqualification.

Checkpoint protocol: Participants must check in at designated checkpoints along the route to verify their progress and receive any necessary information or updates from race officials.

Race officials' decisions: Race officials have the final authority in enforcing the rules and resolving any disputes or concerns that may arise during the race. Participants must comply with their instructions and decisions.
